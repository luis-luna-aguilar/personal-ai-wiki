# Block 1 — State of Play

**Target length:** ~40 minutes.

**Objective for this episode:** Refresh the listener's mental map of where the AI field stands *right now*. By the end, they should be able to answer "who leads in X domain?" and "which frontier models matter?" without checking notes.

**Framing for the podcast generator:**
- Treat this as a briefing, not a product tour. Lead with the current leader per subcategory, note who just lost the lead, and flag where the answer is genuinely ambiguous (multiple co-leaders).
- Dates are load-bearing. Whenever a claim has an `as_of` date, say the date out loud — it lets the listener calibrate freshness.
- Cover the domains in this order: coding, agents, models (general), computer-use, science, legal, finance. Then walk through the frontier models page-by-page.
- Skip benchmark minutiae unless a score *changed the leader*. Benchmarks are evidence, not the story.
- Strip filler: no recaps, no "in today's episode", no outros. Dense, briefing-style.
- If two sources disagree, say so explicitly ("the wiki records both X and Y as leading here") instead of picking one.

**What follows:** the full content of the state-of dashboards, the model pages, and the benchmark pages, concatenated. Use it as the sole source of truth. Do not invent numbers, versions, or leadership claims that aren't present below.
