# Block 2 — Tools & Workflows

**Target length:** ~40 minutes.

**Objective for this episode:** Give the listener a practical shortlist of tools per job-to-be-done, plus the reusable workflow patterns that chain them. After listening, they should know what to reach for when coding, when orchestrating agents, when automating a specific task.

**Framing for the podcast generator:**
- Organize by *job*, not by vendor. Group tools that compete in the same subcategory together and compare them head-to-head.
- For each tool, the listener wants: what it is in one sentence, what it's genuinely good at, where it breaks down, and what competes with it.
- Read pricing and version numbers when they're in the source. Skip marketing adjectives.
- Cover tools first, then workflows. Workflows are the "how to wire them together" layer — explicitly call back to tools by name as they come up.
- Strip filler: no recaps, no "in today's episode", no outros. Dense, practical, hands-on tone.
- If a tool page is thin (e.g. only a status blurb), treat it as a short aside, not a full segment.

**What follows:** the full content of every tool and workflow page, concatenated. Do not invent features, pricing, or integrations that aren't in the source below.
