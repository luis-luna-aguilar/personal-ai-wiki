# AINews — The Two Sides of OpenClaw (harness section)

This AINews issue pulls together several independent signals pointing in the same direction: agent reliability is increasingly a harness problem. The thread running across the cited examples is that strict context boundaries, simpler planning constraints, stronger evals, and better interfaces often deliver more than adding more orchestration complexity.

## Influenced pages

- [[workflows/agentic-orchestration-patterns]] — adds scoped context, failure-aware replanning, and eval-driven simplification as reusable patterns
- [[concepts/harness]] — grounds harness engineering in concrete operational practices rather than abstract definitions

## Key claims extracted

- Published 2026-04-18
- The field is converging on simpler harnesses with stronger evals and cleaner representation layers
- Several cited examples argue many agent failures are instruction and interface failures, not raw model failures
- Strict context boundaries help multi-stage systems remain legible and reliable
- Reliability improvements often come from model-agnostic scaffolding rather than frontier-model chasing
