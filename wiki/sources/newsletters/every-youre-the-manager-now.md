# Every — You’re the Manager Now

Newsletter arguing that the developer role is shifting from direct code production toward supervising parallel agent work, with a related managerial shift in knowledge work more broadly. It also frames AI reliability as a prompt to raise the level of abstraction humans operate at: less mechanical execution, more problem selection, oversight, and judgment.

## Key claims extracted

- As agents get better, the human role shifts toward managing parallel work and reviewing outcomes
- The important interface is increasingly task, context, and preview management rather than raw terminal text
- Better models let humans work at a higher frame by defining the problem, not every step
- AI systems change the job shape from maker-only toward manager-plus-judge
