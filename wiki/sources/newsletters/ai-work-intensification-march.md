# AI work intensification

This source argues that AI often does not reduce work so much as increase the amount of work people feel able, and therefore obligated, to take on. The practical result is task expansion, blurred boundaries, multitasking, and compulsive "one more prompt" behavior that can make AI feel simultaneously energizing and exhausting.

## Key claims extracted

- AI can intensify work by expanding task scope and enabling more parallel obligations
- Prompting feels more like chatting than formal labor, which weakens natural stopping points
- Variable-reward loops and fear of falling behind can make AI use compulsive
- Organizations need boundary-setting and review rhythms, not only access and enthusiasm
