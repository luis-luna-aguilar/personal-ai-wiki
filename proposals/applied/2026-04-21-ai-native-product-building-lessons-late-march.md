## Proven patterns

- **Persistence beats intimidation.** Many non-engineers can cross into building by repeatedly asking the model to explain itself, revising instructions, and iterating through confusion instead of giving up at the first broken prototype
- **Reliability still needs architecture.** Fast prototyping does not remove the need to decide what cannot fail, how information is structured, and which parts of the workflow need engineering-grade reliability

## Failure modes

- **Prototype velocity mistaken for product quality.** Teams can ship something impressive-looking in a day, then discover it has no coherent architecture, weak reliability boundaries, and no clear sense of what cannot fail
```

# AI-native product-building lessons in late March

This source summary groups a late-March cluster on building products with AI. The shared lesson is that AI can remove much of the friction of prototyping, but successful products still depend on coherence, information architecture, persistence, and explicit decisions about reliability.

## Influenced pages

- [[training/company-wide-ai-enablement]] — adds practical guidance on persistence, architecture, and the gap between prototypes and reliable products

## Key claims extracted

- Small teams can now iterate toward product ideas much faster than before
- Non-engineers can often build surprisingly far if they keep asking for explanations and refining instructions
- Product coherence and reliability boundaries still matter even when prototypes come easily
- AI lowers the cost of building the wrong thing just as much as it lowers the cost of building the right thing
```
