# Kimi K2.6

Moonshot AI's latest open-weight model, released April 2026. Specializes in coding, long-horizon execution, and multi-agent coordination. Claims open-source SOTA on major coding and agentic benchmarks.

## Current status (as of 2026-04-21)

- **Architecture:** 1T-parameter MoE, 32B active, 384 experts (8 routed + 1 shared), MLA attention, 256K context, native multimodal, INT4 quantization
- **Claimed open-source SOTA:** HLE w/ tools 54.0, SWE-Bench Pro 58.6, SWE-bench Multilingual 76.7, BrowseComp 83.2
- **Long-horizon execution:** 4,000+ tool calls, 12+ continuous hours demonstrated; 300 parallel sub-agents via Claw Groups
- **Day-0 ecosystem:** vLLM, OpenRouter, Ollama, Cloudflare Workers AI, Baseten, MLX, Hermes Agent, OpenCode
- Kimi claims 68.6% win+tie rate vs Gemini 3.1 Pro on frontend design tasks

## Demonstrated capabilities

- 5-day autonomous infra agent run (monitoring, incident response, system operations)
- Zig inference engine for Qwen3.5-0.8B: 4K+ tool calls, 12 hours, throughput from ~15 to ~193 tokens/sec (+20% over LM Studio)
- exchange-core financial matching engine: 13-hour run, 185% medium throughput gain (0.43 → 1.24 MT/s)

## Strengths

- Long-horizon reliability: enterprise beta testers (Hermes Agent, Augment Code, KiloClaw, OpenCode) consistently report K2.6 > K2.5 on multi-step tasks
- Open weights with strong day-0 inference tooling; practical for self-hosting at scale

## Weaknesses / caveats

- Benchmark re-evaluations are marked with asterisks (*) — Moonshot re-ran competitor evals under their own conditions; treat comparisons as directional
- Anthropic named Moonshot as one of three Chinese labs accused of distillation in February 2026

## Recent changes

- [2026-04-21] Model released and open-sourced; Claw Groups research preview launched
