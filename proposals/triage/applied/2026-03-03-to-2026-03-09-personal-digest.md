- [x] **[training]** AI adoption may intensify work rather than reduce it

    **What it is:** Katie Parrott's March 9 essay is unusually concrete about the psychological and organizational failure modes of heavy AI use. It synthesizes early workplace research into three mechanisms: task expansion, blurred work/life boundaries, and AI-driven multitasking. It then layers in "AI genie" and variable-reward framing to explain why AI work is hard to stop even when it is exhausting. The result is not a product page or generic opinion piece, but an adoption warning: AI can increase scope, context switching, and compulsion unless teams design explicit stopping rules and review rhythms.

    **Why it matters:** This is a good candidate for `training/company-wide-ai-enablement` or `training/anti-autopilot-review-friction`, because it adds a human failure mode the current wiki does not fully capture: AI makes it easier to overwork voluntarily, not only to accept output too easily.
