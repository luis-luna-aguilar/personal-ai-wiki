- [x] **[models]** LeWorldModel — 15M-parameter JEPA model solves representation collapse, trains on single GPU in hours

    **What it is:** A paper called LeWorldModel (LeWM) solves the "representation collapse" problem that previously made JEPA (LeCun's Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture) impractical. Previous implementations needed frozen encoders and massive overhead to prevent the model from collapsing all concepts into one. LeWM uses a single Gaussian regularizer instead. Result: 15M parameters, single-GPU training in hours, plans 48× faster than large foundation world models, intrinsic physics understanding.

    **Why it matters:** If the claims hold, this is a significant datapoint in the generative vs. world-model architecture debate. The source (HowToAI) is promotional in tone — the arXiv paper should be read directly before ingesting.
