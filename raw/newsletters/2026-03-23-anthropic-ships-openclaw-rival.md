The AI leader is [reportedly](https://www.engadget.com/ai/openai-reportedly-plans-to-double-its-workforce-to-8000-employees-161028377.html) expanding its employee count from 4,500 to over 8,000 with hires expected across most departments. ##### **FROM THE FRONTIER**

## **Meet the company using a "cowgorithm" to revolutionize cattle herding **


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/f15139f4-55b6-4e6f-8eb0-59839b36f0b4/superhumanteam_A_robot_herding_cattle_--ar_169_--raw_--sref_h_10394aa4-da75-4184-89f9-899f448bd7e8_2.jpg?t=1774207532)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Using AI to herd cattle. **The internet is [buzzing](https://x.com/MilkRoadAI/status/2035510709124059305) over Halter, a company set to receive funding from Peter Thiel’s VC firm Founders Fund. The product: An AI-powered collar that herds cattle, powered by a proprietary algorithm that’s appropriately nicknamed the cowgorithm.

**How it works.** The solar-powered GPS collars send sound and vibration cues that guide cattle, eliminating the need for fences, farm dogs, or manual herding. The collar also monitors digestion, fertility cycles, and other biometrics — helping farmers get 24/7 coverage of their herd’s health.

**From rockets to pastures.** Halter was founded in 2016 by Craig Piggott, who previously designed spacecraft at Rocket Lab. He quit his job, recruited his former boss as his first investor, and got to work building the company on his family’s farm. Today, over 600,000 cattle wear Halter collars.

**Herding is just the start.** Farmers are aggressively adopting AI to boost efficiency —  from [lasers](https://www.superhuman.ai/p/chatgpt-5-4-is-here-with-built-in-computer-skills#:~:text=Creating%20chemical%2Dfree,bit%20more%20favorable.) that blast weeds to computer vision [systems](https://www.brownfieldagnews.com/news/cargill-uses-ai-to-boost-beef-yields-amid-tight-cattle-supply/) that inspect meat. While the national conversation around AI focuses on office productivity, some of its most tangible gains are showing up in pastures, not cubicles.
