## **It might not be long before you start to see AI in your favorite stores**


--------------------

**AI driving sales.** The data is undeniable: AI is reshaping how we shop online. Per Adobe, AI web traffic to US retailers [jumped](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/16/ai-traffic-to-us-retailers-rose-393-in-q1-and-its-boosting-their-revenue-too/) 393% in Q1 year-over-year — with chatbots like Amazon Rufus and Ask Macy’s driving meaningfully higher conversions. Now AI is getting promoted: from online advisor to in-store assistant.

**Concierge mode.** [Dylan](https://www.modernretail.co/technology/pumas-ai-head-says-the-brand-is-still-giving-the-keys-to-the-consumer-as-it-invests-in-tech-like-a-digital-concierge/) is Puma’s experimental 7-foot "digital human" that speaks 100+ languages and is a running-shoe expert. Guitar Center has also launched [**Rig Advisor**](https://guitar.com/news/music-news/guitar-center-rig-advisor-ai-shopping/), a QR-code shopping assistant, while The Vitamin Shoppe is testing [Shoppe Advisor](https://press.vitaminshoppe.com/2026-01-27-The-Vitamin-Shoppe-R-Opens-New-Innovation-Store-on-New-York-City%E2%80%99s-Upper-East-Side/), an AI touchscreen with expert advice and real-time inventory knowledge.

**Beyond the front desk. **”Concierge” is just one role AI is taking on in retail. Crave Retail recently [debuted](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/crave-retail-exhibits-smart-fitting-room--store-engagement-platform-at-nrf-2026-302657415.html) an AI-powered smart fitting room that lets shoppers request sizes and styling advice while trying on outfits. It’s already in use at Victoria's Secret, Under Armour, and Foot Locker.

**The bottom line.** Retailers are experimenting with AI at every stage of the customer journey. It's already driving traffic and revenue online — so don't be too surprised if AI features start showing up at your favorite stores in the coming months.

##### **PROMPT STATION**

## Comprehensive Document Overview

```
Prompt: [upload document] Act as a highly skilled document analyst with strong comprehension and synthesis abilities. Carefully read the entire uploaded document from start to finish before forming any conclusions, ensuring no sections are overlooked. Begin by clearly identifying the document type (e.g., report, article, proposal), the intended audience, and the primary objective or goal behind the document. Then provide a concise, plain-language executive summary that captures the key ideas, arguments, and outcomes in a way that is easy for a non-expert to understand. After that, break the document down into its major sections or themes, presenting a structured overview where each section includes one to two sentences explaining its purpose and role within the overall document. Finally, distill and highlight the single most important message or takeaway the author wants the reader to remember, ensuring it reflects both the intent and impact of the document.
```
