## **Claude Mythos is one of the most hyped AI models yet — here’s what we know so far.**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/aede8bed-f48a-4b44-af9e-dfc48e4861ee/superhumanteam_A_robot_thats_chained_up_because_its_too_stron_2762b165-089c-438a-96d3-9885a710af07_3.jpg?t=1776189809)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Too strong to release.** Few models have generated as much chatter as Claude Mythos: a model allegedly so powerful that Anthropic is hesitant to release it publicly. Mythos is being kept closely guarded, but a few details have slipped through the cracks.

**It’s a certified hacker. **In [internal testing](https://red.anthropic.com/2026/mythos-preview/), Mythos found and exploited hundreds of software vulnerabilities — even breaking into ten fully up-to-date programs. For reference: Opus 4.6 barely scratched the surface on the same test, finding only a few minor issues. Out in the real world, Mythos has proved just as capable.

**Real-world testing. **The AI Security Institute recently [confirmed](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/our-evaluation-of-claude-mythos-previews-cyber-capabilities) Mythos’ hacking skills, calling its ability to carry out multi-stage attacks a “step up” over previous frontier models. JPMorgan CEO Jamie Dimon agreed, [saying](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/14/jamie-dimon-anthropic-mythos-vulnerabilities-cyber-attacks.html) Mythos uncovered “a lot more vulnerabilities that need to be fixed.” 

**The slow rollout. **Anthropic is slow-playing its Mythos release by giving tech leaders early access to fortify their defenses — an approach that OpenAI is now [mirroring](https://openai.com/index/scaling-trusted-access-for-cyber-defense/). Silicon Valley was built on the mentality of "move fast and break things." But every so often, it's reassuring to see them ease off the gas.
