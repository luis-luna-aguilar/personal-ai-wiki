Most vibe-coded apps die for the same reasons: no persistent
data, no login, no deployment, and no way to handle real users.
The idea is good and the demo is impressive. But nobody can use
it because it lives on your laptop and disappears when you close
the terminal.

I see this constantly. Someone builds something genuinely useful
with Claude Code, shows it off, and gets excited—and then it sits
on their laptop forever. They don't know how to add a database.
They've never set up auth. They're not sure how to get it on the
internet. So the app never becomes a product. It stays a demo
forever.

If that's you, you're not stuck because you're missing some deep
technical skill. You're stuck because no one's walked you through
it yet. The gap is real, but it's way smaller than it feels.
That's why we built this course.
