##### **FROM THE FRONTIER**

## **An agent internet is already under construction — and it could be here much earlier than you think**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/ed2ed25e-bb58-448a-bb21-553d4cbb3668/superhumanteam_A_man_and_a_robot_staring_at_each_other_throug_5a524069-b68d-4bfa-a3c3-0eeccf819e4b_0.jpg?t=1774877788)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Agents enter the workforce.** Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang [estimates](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/jensen-huang-just-painted-most-073100504.html) his company's 75,000 employees will eventually work alongside 7.5 million AI agents — a staggering ratio of 100:1. He's not alone. Last fall, McKinsey [found](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai) that 62% of organizations are already experimenting with AI agents. But there’s one major issue: these new-age workers aren’t designed to work on our internet.

**Early innings.** The global AI agent market was valued at just $5.43B in 2024 — but Precedence Research [expects](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/ai-agents-market) it to reach $236B by 2034, a CAGR of roughly 46%. Right now, many agents are hitching a ride on their owners’ email, computer, or Telegram channel. But for the industry to mature, agents will eventually need their own tools, credentials, and compute.

**Building the agent layer.** Dozens of startups are already hard at work. AgentMail ($6M in funding) gives agents their own email inboxes, while AgentPhone hands them a phone number to call and text from. Both Kite and Sponge let agents make secure payments with their own wallet. And Browserbase (valued at $300M) gives them a browser to freely roam the web.

**The Agentic Web.** Emails. Phone numbers. Wallets. Browsers. Stitch these pieces together and you start to see the early scaffolding for an agent-native internet. Leading researchers call it the [Agentic Web](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.21206) — and most people haven’t noticed it’s under construction.

## **How to Reverse-Engineer Viral Videos with AI**

* Find a viral video on Instagram, TikTok or YouTube (look for high views, saves, and shares)

* Download the video

* Upload it to [Gemini](https://gemini.google.com/)

* Ask Gemini to analyze the video and generate a 30-second script based on the hook, pacing, and structure

**_Sample Prompt:_**_ Analyze this video and break down the hook (first 3 seconds), the structure and pacing, and explain why it works psychologically. Then create a new 30-second script using the same format for this topic: [insert topic]. Make it fast-paced, punchy, and optimized for Instagram/TikTok._

* Gemini will generate a script you can use to create your next viral video
