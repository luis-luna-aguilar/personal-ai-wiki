## **The world's best chess players are gaining an edge by selectively ignoring AI — a skill that’s growing in importance**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/3ce32dcb-a421-4975-a3c2-55033207a5ff/superhumanteam_A_robot_playing_a_business_person_playing_ches_f84edb4a-0af5-49ae-a57a-b92c6f6711e4_2.jpg?t=1774800305)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**AI in chess.** Chess grandmasters have gained surgical precision over their opening sequences thanks to AI tools, often memorizing their first 20+ moves. But when both players prepare with the same tools, they effectively neutralize each other. As a result, draws have [surged](https://www.chess.com/article/view/the-draw-rule-is-classical-chess-dead) at the elite level — until recently.

**Making the wrong move.** Instead of following AI-recommended “perfect” moves, a Bloomberg report shows that champion players deliberately play suboptimal moves. Why? Because it drags opponents into unfamiliar territory where they’re forced to rely on their own critical thinking skills — instead of moves memorized from a computer.

**Staying human.** As the world grows increasingly reliant on AI tools, knowing when to push back will likely become a competitive advantage. Coaxing the best results from AI is important. But so is challenging its answers, thinking creatively, and not always defaulting to ChatGPT’s suggestion.

**Pushing back. **Some of life’s biggest wins can come from decisions an algorithm might consider “non-optimal.” Quitting a job. Pivoting careers. Launching a new venture. Pursuing a different business strategy. When making decisions, lean on AI for guidance and ideas — but remember to also trust your creativity, experience, and instincts. At least that’s what the grandmasters are doing.
