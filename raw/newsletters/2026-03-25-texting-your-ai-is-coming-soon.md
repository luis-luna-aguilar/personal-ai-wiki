**1. Moda launches the first design agent with taste: **A new startup just raised $7.5M to grow its design platform where humans and agents collaborate to create marketing assets, slide decks, and animations. Here’s how it works: the user kicks off the design process with a prompt, a trio of agents does research and creates a final draft, and then the user steps back in to fine-tune the final product. [Watch Moda’s launch video](https://x.com/anvisha/status/2036474296353411290) or try it [here](https://moda.app/).

**2. OpenAI is shutting down the Sora video platform:** The ChatGPT maker [announced](https://x.com/soraofficialapp/status/2036546752535470382) that the popular video generation app and API are being shut down, with more details about timelines and preserving assets coming soon. The move is reportedly part of a larger strategy to focus and redirect resources to higher priorities, such as the [upcoming](https://www.tomsguide.com/ai/openai-just-killed-sora-as-company-readies-ipo-and-new-spud-model) “Spud“ model.

**3. Linq launches the first API to let you text your AI: **Some AI tools (like the viral agent OpenClaw) let you communicate with AI via WhatsApp, Telegram, or a similar messaging platform. [Linq](https://linqapp.com/) wants to go one step further. The startup has raised $27M to unlock conversational experiences on iMessage, SMS, RCS, voice, or even FaceTime — potentially paving the way for texting AI agents. [Watch Linq in action](https://x.com/elliott__potter/status/2036179677014900796).

## **Meta is leaning hard into AI agents — and employees are flocking to one specific tool**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/0035bb8c-e584-4cac-b652-4fe51428c599/superhumanteam_A_company_of_robots_sitting_around_a_board_tab_aa1a656f-8b01-4df0-9c30-03fac89230da_1.jpg?t=1774396019)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Meta’s unofficial pivot.** Meta still calls itself a metaverse company. But in 2026, it’s acting much more like an AI agent company — making strategic acquisitions, reallocating personnel, and encouraging employees at every level to rethink their roles with AI.

**Agents over avatars.** Meta [acqui-hired](https://www.bitget.com/amp/news/detail/12560605295716) AI agent startup Dreamer this week, marking its third agent-focused deal in four months following Manus and Moltbook. Meanwhile, CEO Mark Zuckerberg is building a personal AI agent to help him run the company and has put CTO Andrew Bosworth (who formerly ran metaverse operations) in charge of empowering Meta’s workforce to use AI.

**The favorite tool. **Meta employees are reportedly flocking to** **[MyClaw](https://myclaw.ai/), a personal agent built on OpenClaw but with an easier sign-up process — no terminal or servers required. Some employees are giving their MyClaws access to chat logs and work files, putting those agents in a position to work 24/7. 

**The nail in the coffin? **Meta shut down VR social network Horizon Worlds last week, after Reality Labs [racked](https://sherwood.news/tech/the-metaverses-slow-drawn-out-demise-is-getting-painful/?utm_source=snacks&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=snacks_20260323&utm_content=1eeb2391031682a472cad0d80ef2468a) up roughly $80 billion in cumulative losses since 2020. Now it's pouring $135 billion into AI infrastructure instead. Meta hasn't said the word "pivot" yet — but it sure looks like one.
