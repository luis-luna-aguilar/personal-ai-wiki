## **What is tokenmaxxing? Meet the productivity trend taking over Silicon Valley**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/2cdf1b1a-7ae4-4849-b322-bc4c26863cc4/superhumanteam_Several_robots_working_hard_in_front_of_a_lead_67f83f45-df4c-45a9-ad4a-21b473e9ee9a_0.jpg?t=1776869495)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**The trend.** You’ve probably seen [viral videos](https://x.com/mytechceoo/status/2046664705176187180) or interviews about “tokenmaxxing” — the practice of burning through AI tokens to look as productive as possible. But is it actually a legitimate way to stay ahead? Or just an internet meme?

**The origins.** Tokenmaxxing kicked off when Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang [said](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/jensen-huang-says-nvidia-engineers-should-use-ai-tokens-worth-half-their-annual-salary-every-year-to-be-fully-productive-compares-not-using-ai-to-using-paper-and-pencil-for-designing-chips) he'd be "deeply alarmed" if engineers weren’t burning hundreds of thousands of dollars on tokens. A Meta employee ran with it, building an internal leaderboard to rank which staffers used the most tokens. The thinking: employees using more tokens are more productive. At least on paper. 

**Believers and backlash.** Some companies are taking tokenmaxxing very seriously. Ramp Labs has seen a 13X surge in AI token spend among customers since January, and companies like Uber are [blowing](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/ubers-anthropic-ai-push-hits-223109852.html) through their AI budgets. But other executives are pushing back — arguing the practice is easy to game and rewards token consumption over actual business outcomes.

**Meeting in the middle. **LinkedIn co-founder Reid Hoffman [suggests](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/15/reid-hoffman-weighs-in-on-the-tokenmaxxing-debate/) a middle ground: tracking AI usage matters, but tracking _how _people use it matters more. His advice? Encourage team-wide experimentation, then run regular check-ins to surface what's actually working.
