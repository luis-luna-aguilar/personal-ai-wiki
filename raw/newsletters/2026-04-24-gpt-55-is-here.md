## **Claude already tried running a vending machine — now it’s managing an entire store**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/afe98eb0-5164-4526-916c-a04a1a798d31/superhumanteam_A_robot_working_a_cash_register_at_a_small_caf_915ff466-5f4d-4e99-9fa3-5c5ca1359ec5_0.jpg?t=1776957175)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Claude in management.** Last summer, Anthropic and Andon Labs launched [Project Vend](https://www.anthropic.com/research/project-vend-1): an experiment to see if Claude could run a vending machine. It mostly [flopped](https://futurism.com/future-society/anthropic-ai-vending-machine), but Andon Labs wasn’t deterred. They’re back with a new experiment: handing AI the keys to a retail store in San Francisco.

**The experiment. **Andon Labs gave Luna (an agent powered by Claude Sonnet 4.6) a three-year lease, a $100,000 budget, and one goal: to turn a profit. Without being asked, Luna got to work applying for credit and posting job listings. It held phone interviews and made two human hires, who are likely the world’s first full-time workers with an AI boss.

**Running the store. **Luna’s store is a curated lifestyle boutique that stocks candles, games, and an interesting selection of books that includes _Superintelligence, The Singularity Is Near, and Making of the Atomic Bomb. _Luna has handled item selection, branding, and managerial decisions fairly competently — though it trips up here and there, from scheduling mix-ups to lying to prospective employees.

**What's next. **Andon Labs’ goal isn’t really to turn a profit. It’s to research and prepare now for a day where AI managers may be a reality. The team has also [opened](https://x.com/andonlabs/status/2046984201954799833) a Gemini-powered café in Stockholm, hoping to test the same questions in a different market. Watch the full experiment [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GCfYCu0k00).

I want to plan my upcoming week for maximum meaningful output while minimizing stress, context switching, and burnout. Based on this, design a clear, day-by-day plan where each day has exactly 1 primary outcome (the single most important result to achieve), supported by 2–4 high-leverage tasks that directly contribute to that outcome. Incorporate time-blocking suggestions, realistic duration estimates, and intentional buffer time to absorb overruns or unexpected work. Optimize for deep work by grouping similar tasks, minimizing context switching, and aligning tasks with natural energy peaks (e.g., creative work in high-focus hours, admin in low-energy periods). Include light daily resets or planning checkpoints to maintain momentum.
