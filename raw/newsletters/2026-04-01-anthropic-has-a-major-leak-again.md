## **If you can beat this video game you’re smarter than Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/6300c53e-2930-4ac3-bed1-9b1f93c2eb57/superhumanteam_A_businessman_and_a_robot_playing_video_games__459efa8b-2400-41b0-b36c-17efe54a2bf6_3.jpg?t=1774973332)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Acing exams.** AI models have aced the CPA exam, the bar exam, and other notoriously difficult tests. But according to the ARC-AGI-3 interactive reasoning test, AI still lacks one critical skill: the ability to learn.

**Playing video games. **The ARC-AGI-3 [drops](https://www.revolutioninai.com/2026/03/arc-agi-3-benchmark-ai-scores-openai-spud-anthropic-2026.html) AI models into a video game level with no instructions, rules, or goals — forcing them to figure out both how to play and how to win efficiently. Most humans can solve the test easily. But leading models? Not so much. Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT, and Grok all [scored](https://x.com/scaling01/status/2036853669065306534) less than 1%.

**The knowledge gap. **Today’s most advanced models are essentially incredible memorization machines. They’re trained on immense data sets and then recall that information quickly to answer queries in natural language. But memorization isn’t the same as thinking. That’s why AI can ace an advanced exam it’s been trained on — but struggle when faced with a new video game level.

**Are you smarter than an AI Model? **The ARC-AGI-3 test is open until November, giving AI labs time to work on models that can beat it — and by fall, they very well might. [Play the game](https://arcprize.org/tasks/ls20) yourself to see if you’re smarter than Gemini, Claude, or ChatGPT.
