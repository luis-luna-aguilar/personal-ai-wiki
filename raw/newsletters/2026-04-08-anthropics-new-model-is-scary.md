##### **FROM THE FRONTIER**

## **Choose your company’s AI stance fast — before your customers choose for you.**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/293f2304-0952-4853-8868-34b54643bc26/superhumanteam_A_crowd_of_picketers_split_into_two_equal_side_3fb8f646-0c80-4152-94b4-631005d85d27_3.jpg?t=1775608381)
Caption: 


--------------------
**The anti-AI consumer.** Half of US consumers would rather buy from brands that _don't_ use generative AI in ads, messaging, or content — and companies are starting to pick sides. "Made by humans" is quickly becoming the next organic, non-GMO, or handmade.

**Leading the charge.** Companies like [**Aerie**](https://www.ae.com/ca/en/content/aerie/aerie-real), Equinox, Almond Breeze, and **[Polaroid](https://press.polaroid.com/251772-polaroid-s-new-campaign-pushes-back-against-the-reign-of-screens-and-ai-and-celebrates-analog/)**** **have turned “anti-AI” into a marketing strategy — from public callouts to full-blown ad campaigns. Gartner analyst Emily Weiss [**puts**](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-03-16-gartner-marketing-survey-finds-50-percent-of-consumers-prefer-brands-that-avoid-using-genai-in-consumer-facing-content0) it bluntly: "marketers should treat GenAI as a trust decision as much as a technology decision."

**But there’s a catch. **The "No AI" badge isn’t about avoiding AI completely — it’s about being [**transparent**](https://wfanet.org/knowledge/item/2026/04/02/global-brands-call-for-clearer-consensus-on-ai-labelling-as-usage-accelerates-wfa-research#:~:text=Eighty%2Dtwo%20percent%20of%20multinationals%20believe%20transparency%20is%20essential%20for%20protecting%20brand%20reputation) with customers. Using AI to create a simple background? Only 4% think that needs a label. But using an AI voice to chat with customers? 96% say that needs a disclosure. When in doubt, just remember this rule of thumb: don't pretend a machine is a human.

**Pick your lane. **Brands using AI unapologetically are winning on volume, speed, and cost. Those staying deliberately human-first are winning on customer goodwill. But the ones in the middle? They’re slowly losing on both ends.

## **How to turn any flat image into an editable design**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/3c78e445-d2a8-4a43-b452-c5ac78f3f63b/Figma_Group_1000005176.jpg?t=1775639221)
Caption: 


--------------------
* Open [Canva](https://www.canva.com/) → go to Magic Layers, click Select Media, and upload your image

* Wait ~30–60 seconds. Canva automatically detects layout, text, objects, and background, and separates everything into editable layers

* Click any layer to edit instantly. Update text, fix typos, change dates, or tweak taglines without rebuilding the design

* Select object layers to move, resize, duplicate, or remove elements with full control

* Drag in a new image (product shot, logo, etc.). It snaps into the existing layout automatically

* Fine-tune the design by adjusting colors using the color picker to match your brand or style
