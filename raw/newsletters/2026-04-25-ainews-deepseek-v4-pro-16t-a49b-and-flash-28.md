# [AINews] DeepSeek V4 Pro (1.6T-A49B) and Flash (284B-A13B), Base and Instruct — runnable on Huawei Ascend chips

**From:** AINews <swyx+ainews@substack.com>

After a couple months’ delay and lots of speculation, DeepSeek finally released the heavily anticipated DSV4 [ https://substack.com/redirect/9ba15800-e81f-45f0-b086-f8132e85098b?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ], the first major version model since DSV3 (Dec 2024) and DSR1 (Jan 2025). It brings the DeepSeek family up in line with Kimi K2.6 [ https://substack.com/redirect/09ccebc4-18d4-4a12-bfc9-548e0947f6f5?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ], the current open model leader, and Xiaomi Mimo 2.5 [ https://substack.com/redirect/d531311d-cf70-4c98-a3da-1bd5eba0f43c?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ], a lesser known family released 2 days ago [ https://substack.com/redirect/d55114b9-ed1d-4dc3-9250-c9e7c4f61f36?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ].
The DSV4 family is roughly a Gemini 3.1, GPT 5.4, Opus 4.6 level model, up to 1.6T MOE withtrained on 32T tokens with FP4 [ https://substack.com/redirect/c17fe1c2-aa04-4a9a-8a1e-83a483853e55?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ], with 1M token context (supported by their new Compressed Sparse Attention (CSA) and Heavily Compressed Attention (HCA) techniques), and incredibly rarely, they released both the Base and Instruct versions - surely setting the stage for a possible “DeepSeek R2” in future, though this one already has reasoning effort.
The technical report [ https://substack.com/redirect/9ca93308-ef9f-4687-8cff-a820e618987a?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] is a typically dense 58 pages, demonstrating training and inference insights and improvements from the Manifold Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC) paper [ https://substack.com/redirect/a9687fac-b2dd-40a3-8c04-26b3c0485b80?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] they released in January, continued usage of Moonshot’s Muon [ https://substack.com/redirect/e0c96ac5-9929-4d97-8a3b-7baf041ea207?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ], and CSA/HCA’s overall INCREDIBLE efficiency improvements on DeepSeek 3.2-Exp’s already impressive Sparse Attention [ https://substack.com/redirect/a94e2df9-a4b2-4cf4-bed4-990ee29b68e0?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] - at 1M tokens, requiring only 27% of FLOPs and 10% of KV cache memory compared with DeepSeek-V3.2:
The geopolitical backdrop behind the Huawei CANN compatibility [ https://substack.com/redirect/ab49efae-e35a-4547-8dd3-4fc5c00fd905?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] is DeepSeek weaning dependence off export-controlled NVIDIA/CUDA chips — Ascends are still a quarter the supply [ https://substack.com/redirect/015e57b5-634d-40cc-9405-739be18ab782?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] of H100s, but this is an important milestone for Chinese total independence.

AI Twitter Recap
Top Story: DeepSeek V4
DeepSeek released DeepSeek-V4 Pro and DeepSeek-V4 Flash, its first major architecture refresh since V3 and first clear two-tier lineup, with 1M-token context, hybrid reasoning/non-reasoning modes, an MIT license, and a technical report detailed enough that multiple researchers called it one of the most important or best-written model papers of the year. Across the reactions, the factual consensus is that V4 materially advances open-weight long-context and agentic coding performance while remaining somewhat behind the top closed frontier models overall. Independent benchmarkers place V4 Pro around the #2 open-weights tier, roughly near Kimi K2.6 / GLM-5.1 / strong Claude Sonnet-class to Opus-ish depending on benchmark and mode, with especially strong long-context and agentic performance; opinions diverge on how close it is to GPT-5.x / Opus 4.7 and on whether this is “democratizing” progress or an architecture so complex that few open labs can realistically reproduce it. Broader implications
Three implications stand out.
Open-weight long-context is no longer just marketing.
V4’s strongest contribution may be proving that 1M context can be made operationally credible in an open-weight model, with concrete KV-cache engineering and open inference support.
