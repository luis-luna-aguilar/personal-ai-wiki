It is Cloud Next today and Google TPUv8’s (training and inference iterations) were announced as expected [ https://substack.com/redirect/394ee21a-c362-4fb3-adf3-c39eb79ee333?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ], though the numbers are mindboggling, they mostly serve to reinforce the sheer hardware advantage that a decade of investment has given to GDM and any models they train and serve.
Over the last 2 days with AIE Miami [ https://substack.com/redirect/5a162853-f51f-4a91-9893-0a16dd252568?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] concluding (Singapore [ https://substack.com/redirect/f7a2f688-185d-44d9-bcac-848e8aff956a?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] is next!) the top conversations we have been hearing from AI leadership (CTOs, VPs, Founders) have all centered around the concept of “Tokenmaxxing” and how leaders want to get their teams using more AI, WITHOUT the downside of incentivizing the kinds of horrendous waste our friend Gergely Orosz  described at his AIE keynote [ https://substack.com/redirect/aab37381-b692-4bb4-ba6f-a5a1881c1e03?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ].
Dex Horthy, coiner of Context Engineering and “the Dumb Zone”, publicly retracted  [ https://substack.com/redirect/a2fbab53-9a9e-4382-be4f-d065ef21602e?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ]his extremely vibe-coding-pilled call 6 months ago and encouraged people to please read the code, citing Alex Volkov ’s Z/L continuum from AIE Europe [ https://substack.com/redirect/52bbeb21-8c59-45b6-a0e3-ec8e96e3e41c?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ]:
Off the record, many senior leaders I talk to are more on the Zechner side [ https://substack.com/redirect/3c38b5d0-a885-416b-9754-ee2cb46564bb?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] than the Lopopolo side [ https://substack.com/redirect/ee0b3c7c-6362-4486-99ca-925def4a8224?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] of the Z/L spectrum — this does not mean that one side is true for every one in every situation, nor does it mean it will continue to be true with advancing model progress! To point out the most obvious, engineers and engineering leaders are the ones most setup to make a big deal out of minor architectural quality issues that sheer quantity of cheap code generation and code review might overcome.
Today’s LS guest, Mikhail Parakhin, CTO of Shopify, had another take on the “tasteful tokenmaxxing” - you want to go for depth (e.g. do more serial autoresearch loops) than go for breadth (e.g. solve a problem by kicking off 5, 10, 50, 500 parallel runs of the LLM slot machine). Worth thinking through.

Traces/evals/self-improvement are becoming the core agent data primitive: The strongest thread here came from LangChain-adjacent discussion. @Vtrivedy10 [ https://substack.com/redirect/682e31c1-8375-4c11-b15d-7de270975ed7?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] argued that traces capture agent errors and inefficiencies, and that compute should be pointed at understanding traces to generate better evals, skills, and environments; a longer follow-up [ https://substack.com/redirect/ca6982c9-46cd-4aeb-8dc2-20cf722313d7?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] expanded this into a concrete loop involving trace mining, skills, context engineering, subagents, and online evals. @ClementDelangue [ https://substack.com/redirect/6dddf35a-6b0e-4069-a8ac-deb8b504faa8?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] pushed for open traces as the missing data substrate for open agent training, while @gneubig [ https://substack.com/redirect/b8a1ac76-e936-40b5-8f5c-d2501c6f692f?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] promoted ADP / Agent Data Protocol standardization. Post-Training, RL, and Inference Systems
Perplexity and others shared more of the post-training playbook: @perplexity_ai [ https://substack.com/redirect/afa436e3-ed81-4582-b77f-b66c5c14eed6?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] published details on a search-augmented SFT + RL pipeline that improves factuality, citation quality, instruction following, and efficiency; they say Qwen-based systems can match or beat GPT-family models on factuality at lower cost. @AravSrinivas [ https://substack.com/redirect/a3a28f7d-5888-46a7-9552-4b7eaabd7c20?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] added that Perplexity now runs a post-trained Qwen-derived model in production that unifies tool routing and summarization and is already serving a significant share of traffic. On the research side, @michaelyli__ [ https://substack.com/redirect/dc825f42-e423-43bf-ae1a-b046fbd0c9b8?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] introduced Neural Garbage Collection, using RL to jointly learn reasoning and KV-cache retention/eviction without proxy objectives; @sirbayes [ https://substack.com/redirect/638b3242-ae4d-4818-be25-b6fa75873c1d?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] reported a Bayesian linguistic-belief forecasting agent matching human superforecasters on ForecastBench.
The “minimal editing” problem in coding models got a useful benchmark treatment: @nrehiew_ [ https://substack.com/redirect/2af2b698-b730-4216-a9ff-f74ebb362d0c?j=eyJ1IjoiODlhdDlmIn0.psR-epQqK_Wbg6RTI-yQkSgRmDIqosMhU9elL-EoqO0 ] presented work on Over-Editing, where coding models fix bugs by rewriting too much code.
