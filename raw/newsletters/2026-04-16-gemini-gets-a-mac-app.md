##### **FROM THE FRONTIER**

## **Allbirds announced one of the most unexpected business pivots ever — and its stock soared 600+%**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/28983d3b-8d27-40f4-a71c-24a8befc8f2b/superhumanteam_A_pair_of_Allbirds_sneakers_in_front_of_a_soar_27e62920-10f8-4ee8-b8bb-048b966ec7fb_2.jpg?t=1776266300)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Remember Allbirds? **The shoemaker built a cult-like following in the early 2020s with its eco-friendly wool shoes. It went public in 2021 and shares [surged](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/15/allbirds-bird-stock-shoes-ai.html) 90% on day one — but it’s been all downhill since then. Sales slumped, shares followed, and the stock dropped from $20 to $2.5. But the brand has a new business plan to turn things around: AI compute infrastructure.

**Sole searching. **Allbirds has raised $50M and is [spinning](https://ir.allbirds.com/news-releases/news-release-details/allbirds-inc-executes-50m-convertible-financing-facility) up a fresh entity to pursue its new venture. Name? NewBird AI. Mission statement? To become a “fully integrated GPU-as-a-Service (GPUaaS) and AI-native cloud solutions provider.” Translation: buying and leasing compute for AI.

**Still selling shoes. **Don’t sprint to an Allbirds outlet just yet. The company sold its intellectual property to a brand management firm for $39M, and the new owner plans to keep products on shelves. Not literal shelves though — the company recently [shuttered](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/28/allbirds-stores-retail.html) all but two stores.

**Bird's-eye view. **This type of headline feels familiar. Like when Long Island Iced Tea [rebranded](https://www.cnbc.com/2017/12/21/long-island-iced-tea-micro-cap-adds-blockchain-to-name-and-stock-soars.html) to Long Blockchain during Bitcoin's boom. Or when countless retailers tacked ".com" onto their names in the early-2000s. Only time will tell if NewBird is capitalizing on a major market — or the canary in the AI coal mine.

## **How to Use Chrome’s New AI-Powered ‘Skills’**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/0ac836ad-80a0-4ee5-97df-396a620e1f1a/Figma_Group_1000005185.jpg?t=1776254099)
Caption: 


--------------------
* Go to ‘[Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/category/extensions)’ and download the ‘Gemini in Chrome’ extension

* Once done, login with your Gemini account and start using the Gemini sidebar

* Write any prompt you want to reuse (e.g., “Summarize this page” or “Turn this into a LinkedIn post”)

* After sending the prompt save it as a ‘Skill’ and give it a clear name

* When browsing any webpage, trigger your saved Skills by typing ‘/’ or ‘+’ and select your saved Skill from the list

* The Skill will automatically execute your saved prompt on that content

* Use Skills for repetitive tasks like summarizing articles, drafting emails, analyzing tabs, or generating posts
