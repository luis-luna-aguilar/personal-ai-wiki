## **The duality of AI: thoughts from 80,000 Claude users**


--------------------
View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/505cbb94-a915-4670-bc7a-3b6b34322a0b/superhumanteam_A_crowd_of_people_taking_a_written_test_--ar_1_ac3dba8f-8254-4131-ab8d-415d8f201ec2_2.jpg?t=1773877126)
Caption: Made with Midjourney


--------------------
**Anthropic’s sentiment study. **Anthropic just published one of the largest qualitative [studies](https://www.anthropic.com/features/81k-interviews) ever on how people experience AI around the globe. The goal: understand how people use it, what they want from it — and what they fear about it.

**Conflicted feelings.** The answers [revealed](https://www.anthropic.com/features/81k-interviews#:~:text=What%20people%20want%20from%20AI%20and,this%20varied%20by%20stated%20job%20category.) an interesting trend: people’s wants and fears were often closely tied. Respondents who benefited from AI in a given area regularly voiced concerns about that same benefit — a trend that showed up consistently across regions and subjects.

**The data. **33% of respondents said they enjoy using AI to learn new subjects, yet 17% were fearful they’ll eventually stop thinking for themselves. 28% cited economic empowerment as a benefit, while 18% dread job displacement. And people who lean on AI for emotional support? They were 3X more likely to fear becoming dependent on it.

**The good news.** Benefits tended to be grounded in real experience, while fears were more hypothetical. Either way, it’s clear that people aren't neatly divided into AI optimists and pessimists — they're often both, simultaneously benefiting from AI while worrying about its long-term impacts. Read [hundreds of interview quotes](https://www.anthropic.com/features/81k-interviews#quotes) from the study.
