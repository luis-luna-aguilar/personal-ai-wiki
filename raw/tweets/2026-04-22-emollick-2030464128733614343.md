I gave ChatGPT for Excel and Claude for Excel a try on a very hard Excel file: macro-economic data from 1,000 years of English history across over a hundred tabs.
I think both did a good job, and I did not spot errors (though I only did spot checks). However, Claude was harder to check because ChatGPT tended to stick within the Excel app, building formulas and manipulating the data in the way a person would. On the other hand, Claude used Python and often pasted material into Excel for display purposes only, making it harder to trace or edit. If that holds, I think it will generally make ChatGPT more useful for serious users if you want to audit the results.
