Personal notes & takes

The personal/ folder is a separate space for your own opinions, philosophies, journal-style notes, and subjective takes. It is not part of the factual wiki. The LLM treats it with different rules.

Why separate it?

The factual wiki is about what's true now, with dates and sources. Your personal takes are about what you think — they shouldn't decay the same way, and they shouldn't be rewritten by incoming sources. Keeping them separate means:

  • A new paper doesn't overwrite your opinion because it "contradicts" you.
  • Your takes don't get flagged as stale if you haven't updated them in a month.
  • The LLM won't auto-update personal pages from sources — you drive those updates explicitly.
  • The factual wiki stays clean, citeable, and dateable.

Structure

personal/
  philosophies/    # long-form beliefs about how AI should be used
    compound-engineering.md
    context-is-everything.md
    agent-native-defaults.md
  takes/           # shorter, more opinionated commentary
    why-rag-is-overrated.md
    cursor-vs-claude-code-for-me.md

The split between philosophies/ and takes/ is soft — use whichever fits. Philosophies are stable and load-bearing; takes are spikier and may change.

Linking rules

Links between personal/ and wiki/ are one-way:

  • Allowed: personal → wiki. A philosophy page can link to tools/cursor.md, concepts/rag.md, etc.
  • Not allowed: wiki → personal. Wiki pages never link to personal content. The factual wiki stays uncontaminated by opinion.

This means you can cite facts from your takes, but your takes don't leak into the canonical dashboards.

How the LLM treats personal pages

  • No automatic updates. A new source never modifies a personal page — the LLM won't even propose it. Personal updates happen only when you ask directly.
  • Staleness ignored. stale.py skips the personal/ folder by default (controlled via config.yml → stale.exclude_paths).
  • No auto-creation. The LLM never creates personal pages on its own. If it thinks an opinion piece is worth capturing, it asks you first.
  • Voice preserved. When you do ask for edits, the LLM keeps your phrasing — it's not allowed to rewrite tone or flatten strong opinions.

Using meeting notes and personal raw files

Two raw/ folders feed this space:

  • raw/meetings/ — transcripts or notes from meetings you were in.
  • raw/notes/ — your own written notes, jottings, voice memos transcribed.

When you drop something in these folders and ask to ingest, the default target is personal/ — usually personal/takes/ or a new personal page — not the wiki. If you explicitly want something in the wiki instead, say so:

ingest raw/notes/2026-04-09-thoughts-on-cursor.md but file the factual parts into the wiki

The LLM will split facts from opinions and propose wiki updates for the facts, leaving the opinions in a personal page.

Editing personal pages directly

You can always just edit personal pages in Obsidian yourself — no need to involve the LLM. For assistance, tell it what to do:

  • "edit personal/philosophies/compound-engineering.md to add a section on X"
  • "clean up the phrasing in personal/takes/why-rag-is-overrated.md but keep my voice"

Personal edits don't go through the proposal flow — the LLM applies them directly (since you're the only author). If the edit is substantial, ask for a diff preview first.

Citing personal content in conversation

When you ask a question, the LLM draws on the wiki by default. You can pull in personal context explicitly:

  • "based on my philosophy pages, would Cursor or Claude Code fit my workflow better?"
  • "reconcile my take on RAG with what the wiki says"

The LLM will read both spaces and be explicit about which claims come from which.
